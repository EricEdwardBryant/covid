What do I think is the most relevant information?

For me (and my friends, and my family) I think the most important thing is to be aware of COVID-19 prevalence in our communities. Fortunately, CA publishes new positive SARS-CoV-2 tests every day with a county level breakdown. This is an imperfect metric and should be considered an underestimate of community prevalence because it only catches cases that were actually tested. Reporting is also a bit off as we can see strong weekly periodicity when we visualize the raw data, which is why I include a two week rolling average and a smooth curve when plotting daily new cases.

That said, the smoothed curves are also likely underestimating community prevalence as the under-reporting on certain days drags the whole average down. The more important thing to pay attention to is whether daily new cases are increasing, stable, or decreasing. Increases in daily new cases means that every new cases is resulting in more than one additional new case, such that spread is no longer being controlled. Decreasing daily new cases indicates that our control measures are working.

Why don’t you include daily new deaths?

Want more data? See the dashboards linked above! That said, be careful when interpreting daily new deaths. This measure lags by several weeks behind daily new cases. While I think it is important to keep in mind that many people have died and continue to die from this disease, I do not think monitoring daily new deaths helps individuals assess the current prevalence of COVID-19 in their communities, and thus does not inform us how to act today to avoid getting and/or spreading COVID-19.

Also, be aware that daily new deaths (i.e. new reported deaths yesterday) is not the same thing as infection fatality rate (IFR i.e. rate of death among infected persons). IFR is something that you should probably only worry about if you get COVID-19 and even then, you would want the IFR for someone of your same demographic with the same co-morbidities etc. And, even then, it still is a poor indicator for how you will respond to COVID-19. IFR is something more relevant to policy makers and people running clinical trials that they use, along with many other important factors, to help assess the current standard of care and the status of our healthcare system. So, instead of worrying about crude death rates, or IFR, listen to public health experts when they say this is a serious disease that needs to be managed and not ignored.

What is the “loess estimate”?

LOESS stands for “LOcally Estimated Scatterplot Smoothing,” and is just the algorithm I used to fit smooth curves (i.e. make the line pretty). This estimated curve is also used to annotate the numbers directly on the plot that provide to tell us the approximate peak and current value for daily new cases. The exact numbers should not be taken too seriously, as they are almost certainly underestimates of actual COVID-19 prevalence. That said these estimates will not fluctuate as wildly as the raw reported daily new cases – this is intentional as these fluctuations are almost certainly reporting artifacts that should be ignored.
